def color_map(time): #Takes in a time and returns a corresponding color and icon name for the marker
    time = int(time[0:2])
    if time in range(6, 12): #Morning: 6 AM - Noon
        return ["blue", "sun-o"]
    elif time in range(12, 19): #Afternoon: Noon - 7 PM
        return ["lightred", "fire"]
    elif time in range(19, 21): #Evening: 7 PM - 9 PM
        return ["orange", "cloud"]
    else: #Night: 9 PM - 6 AM
        return ["black", "moon-o"]

map_osm = folium.Map(location=[39.29, -76.61], zoom_start=11) #Creates map and centers it at Baltimore
for index, row in arrest_table.iterrows():
    if row["arrestDate"][0:2] == "12" and ( #Checks if arrest happened in December
        #The following 3 conditions check if the arrest was related to some form of theft
        "Theft" in str(row["chargeDescription"]) or 
        "Burglary" in str(row["chargeDescription"]) or
        "Larceny" in str(row["chargeDescription"])
    ):
        inputs = color_map(row["arrestTime"]) #Retrieves corresponding icon color and name
        folium.Marker( #Creates icon and adds it to the map
            location=[row["lat"], row["long"]],
            popup=row["arrest"],
            icon=folium.Icon(color=inputs[0], icon=inputs[1], prefix="fa")
        ).add_to(map_osm)

'''
Considering how much people talk about the "spirit of Christmas", I wanted to see how effective this was at preventing
the crime opposite of what Santa is famous for: theft. Additionally, do most of these thefts occur in broad daylight,
when Santa's gaze is long gone, or in the black of night, where most are too busy spending time with family to guard
storefronts and property?

What this map seeks to uncover is the location and general time of day that thefts tend to occur in the Baltimore
area, specifically during the holiday season. This was accomplished by restricting the data that got placed on the map
to only arrests that:

1.) Took place during December
2.) Included some form of theft

From there, arrest points are given unique colors and icons based on what time the arrest occurred, designated as
follows:

Morning: 6 AM - Noon, Blue Sun
Afternoon: Noon - 7 PM, Light Red Fire
Evening: 7 PM - 9 PM, Orange Cloud
Night: 9 PM - 6 AM, Black Moon

Most arrests took place during the afternoon (light red appears the most frequently) and on streets (most arrests
take place on the orange roads). This could imply midday siestas and easy access to roads for getaway vehicles are 
conducive for grand theft but more research would be necessary to substantiate this hypothesis.
'''
